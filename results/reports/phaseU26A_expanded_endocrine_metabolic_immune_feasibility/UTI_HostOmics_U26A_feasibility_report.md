## Ranked high-impact research questions

1. **RQ2** (testable_now_high_priority; score 1.0): Does UTI induce an inflammatory carbon-use state integrating glycolysis, lactate-HIF1A, pentose-phosphate, insulin-IRS and mTOR signaling while suppressing oxidative metabolism?
2. **RQ5** (testable_now_high_priority; score 1.0): Do insulin, IRS and adipokine programs link pregnancy-associated inflammation, carbohydrate metabolism and recurrent UTI susceptibility?
3. **RQ6** (testable_now_high_priority; score 1.0): Do tryptophan-kynurenine, arginine-nitric-oxide, glutamine and one-carbon programs define immune activation and nutrient-handling states relevant to UPEC persistence?
4. **RQ3** (testable_now_high_priority; score 0.9922): Which complement initiation and amplification route dominates recurrent UTI, pregnancy-associated UTI, urine inflammatory states and UPEC-responsive single cells?
5. **RQ1** (testable_now_high_priority; score 0.9813): Do pregnancy-associated UTI states shift from progesterone-supportive and steroid-conversion programs toward androgenic, glucocorticoid, inflammatory or steroid-catabolic programs?
6. **RQ4** (testable_now_high_priority; score 0.9813): Do lipid remodeling, inflammatory eicosanoids and ferroptosis-linked lipid peroxidation connect UTI inflammation with altered steroid precursor availability?
7. **RQ7** (testable_now_high_priority; score 0.88): Are catecholamine-adjacent stress, iron-sequestration and redox programs linked to UPEC-responsive inflammatory biology?
8. **RQ8** (testable_now_high_priority; score 0.88): Does UTI activate nucleotide turnover, NAD consumption, purine oxidation and nitrogen-handling programs that track inflammatory-cell proliferation and oxidative stress?

## Statistical decision rules for the next phase

- Use differential submodule activity with effect sizes and FDR-controlled inference as the default.
- Use logistic regression and odds ratios only when a true binary outcome is present and group sizes are adequate.
- Prefer AUROC with permutation testing when odds ratios are unstable or sample sizes are modest.
- Build module-module and partial-correlation networks by tissue/context rather than pooling biologically incompatible layers.
- In single-cell analyses, validate pathway activity within cell populations and at sample-aggregated pseudobulk level.
- Describe glycolysis and related transcriptomic outputs as transcriptionally inferred metabolic pathway activity, not actual flux.
